## Freedom Badge

In front of the Pokemon Center in Celeste City is another Pokemon Breeder, who you can use for Experience grinding up to the level cap of 30. You'll get *3 Exp. Candy S* for each win.

!battle(["Jim",:POKEMANIAC,0], "Grassy Terrain")

Talk to Aderyn nearby her gym, then head in. Use the machine to reach the cloud layer.

!battle(["Daisy",:PICNICKER,0], "Cloud Field")

Talk to the bottom left totem first, then the winged totem in the center. Touch the top left totem, then make your way around the right side to battle another trainer.

!battle(["Benny",:YOUNGSTER,0], "Cloud Field")

Touch the nearby, top-right totem and proceed up.

!battle(["Joshy",:COOLTRAINER_M,0], "Cloud Field")

Head all the way to the bottom of the map and talk to the winged totem to clear another crystal wall. Finally, talk to the center left totem then the top right totem to reach the top of the map, where you can talk to the final winged totem.

!battle(["Aderyn",:ADERYN,0], "Cloud Field")

This is another very difficult gym battle, in no small part thanks to the lack of options on the player's side once again. The field suppresses Electric moves and especially Rock moves while boosting many of Aderyn's moves like Fly and Ominous Wind. She has a good number of anti-sweep mechanisms in place - Altaria shuts down weather and gets defensive boosts thanks to Cloud Nine, Xatu gets a free turn of Protect thanks to its seed, and Swellow is extremely fast and packs Quick Attack to boot. Gligar and Swanna round out the standard Flying type weaknesses, meaning only a diverse team that can overcome the difficulties of the field can succeed.

One obvious plus in the field notes is that Forecast grants Speed and Defenses upon switch in. Combine this with extra-long Hail and Rain Dance, and you can see how Castform can be an actual threat in this fight as long as you have an answer for Altaria. 

For winning, you'll get the Freedom Badge, bringing the level cap up to 40, as well as *TM40 Aerial Ace*.

# Episode 1: TODO

Welcome to my 100% walkthrough of Pokemon Desolation.

My aim with this document is to provide a complete route through the game including every single item including hidden ones, available Pokemon, some tips for battle strategies, shop and tutor details, and more. To summarize my approach: if you're the type of person that doesn't really like having tons of tabs open at once while you're trying to 100% complete this game, hopefully my guide may be useful for you.

Since this guide is complete, there are some parts that may spoil events that happen later in the game. I try to be vague about story details, but you may want to consider playing the game once first without referencing this walkthrough if this is a concern.

## A Well Deserved Holiday

You'll begin by choosing a gender, character spriteset, and name. Next, the game will ask you if you want to input any passwords into the system. For a first-time playthrough, you don't need to worry about these, but you can peruse the full list of [passwords](#full-password-list) if you wish.

You can interact with objects in the room once you're given control. In particular, you may want to read up on the Ayrith region via interacting with the sparkle in the bookshelf.

Get familiarized with the game's controls and menus. Worth noting via looking at the trainer card is that there are hard level caps in this game, meaning your Pokemon will not pass the current level cap, which for now, is Lv. 20.

Enter the room to the left to talk to Scarlett. This is our first introduction to "Relationship Points" in Desolation, where you can improve or worsen your relationships with other characters depending on your choices, mostly through dialogue. I only list interactions in this guide that have an effect on these points.

**Relationship Point Choices**:
- Yes (+1 Scarlett)

You can read up on more information via the back bookshelf. Take the time to explore this floor of the ship. In general, talking to everyone and reading every book you can find is a good idea in this game.

Once you're done exploring, head up the stairs in the central room and attempt to head up past Ava and Scarlett. After a scene, you'll be free to explore this level of the ship.

**Relationship Point Choices**:
- Talk to Ava on the western deck (+1 Ava)

Head through the doors at the top of the VIP room to talk to the Captain. After a chat, you'll next need to return to the eastern side of the first floor and enter the bottom-left set of doors to talk to Connor. Follow Connor into the room to the west, then head out the bottom of the room into the basement. Talk to Connor, then quickly escape the basement. Talk to the sailor at the top of the next room to proceed.

This ends the prologue, and begins Act 1: Beginnings!

## Keneph Beach

Follow Connor into the grove, then activate the healing machine. We can return to use this machine anytime. Outside, Connor will gift you *10 Poke Balls*! We'll need to use one to catch our first partner Pokemon. Though they're not the usual set of starters, there are some good picks here; choose from the following starter Pokemon options: **Venipede**, **Pancham**, **Yamper**, **Phanpy**, **Spheal**, **Doduo**, **Skiddo**, or **Houndour**. 

It's worth noting that all Pokemon in this game can be caught with their Hidden Abilities, just as likely as their normal abilities, and that Ability Capsules allow switching between all abilities freely.

To the east, there is some grass. You can use the Poke Balls we obtained earlier to catch some new team members, though in the early game balls are sparse, so be careful. It'll also be a long time before we can access a PC, so don't send a team member to the PC unless you're ready to be without it for a while.

!enc(440, "Grass")

I will point out that Zigzagoon can come with [Pickup](#pickup-table). Desolation is *extremely* stingly with money in the early game, so if you want to complete all the sidequests and catch all the Pokemon, using Pickup to get stuff to sell is going to be essential. Poke-Candy in particular tends to eat up your money for how many Pokemon need them, so picking those up is essential.

You'll battle trainers as you travel eastward. You'll note a weird message at the beginning of each battle: Desolation imports Reborn and Rejuvenation's Field Effect system! We don't have any in-game documentation of these for now, though at the moment they shouldn't come into play too much.

This is a really tough fight this early into the game. The game's first real challenge resulting from field effects comes in the form of Roselia's Nature Power, which becomes Rock Tomb and gets a boost on this field, while almost all of the team members we have access to at this point are weak to Rock. Aipom and Sneasel may have gotten evolutions later in Pokemon's history, but are still statted in such a way that they're very difficult to survive and take out so early into the game. You may need to grind your team up with Connor a bit more to beat this fight.

## Redcliff Town

Once we're let in, we finally gain access to the familiar creature comforts: a Pokemon Center with a PC, a Poke Mart, etc. The bush to the left of the Pokemon Center contains a hidden *Oran Berry*. In this house, you can perform an in-game trade: your Lombre, for a **Budew**! Outside, you can talk to Scarlett if you want for our first "dilemma" in relationship points:

**Relationship Point Choices**:
- Say it is possible (+1 Scarlett, -1 Connor)
- Say it isn't possible (+1 Connor, -1 Scarlett)

Inside a rock south of the battlefield is a hidden *Chesto Berry*. There is also a shop here:

!shop("Redcliff Shop", ["Poke Ball", "Potion", "Antidote", ["Poke-Candy", 2000], "Reverse Candy"])

Head into the town hall on the eastern side of town. Talk to the girl on the right side to obtain **Skitty**! The boy nearby will give us a *Great Ball*. Finally, talk to the man at the top to progress.

Our next destination is the lab at the top of town. Inside the lab, head left to follow the professor upstairs. You'll actually get to pick one of the twenty-four *real* starter Pokemon this time! Choose from:

- The Grass types: **Bulbasaur**, **Chikorita**, **Treecko**, **Turtwig**, **Snivy**, **Chespin**, **Rowlet**, **Grookey**
- The Fire types: **Charmander**, **Cyndaquil**, **Torchic**, **Chimchar**, **Tepig**, **Fennekin**, **Litten**, **Scorbunny**
- The Water types: **Squirtle**, **Totodile**, **Mudkip**, **Piplup**, **Oshawott**, **Froakie**, **Popplio**, **Sobble**

Back downstairs, utilize the dark machine, the Beamer Grid, to get a feel for the teleportation network. We can't go anywhere else yet, but it's something to remember for the future. 

Enter the room on the right next, and talk to the man to begin the **Research Assistant** sidequest. He wants to see a Bibarel. Leveling a Bidoof to Lv. 15 to get **Bibarel** and showing it to him will net you a *Exp. Candy M*. That's all we can do for now in this quest.

Talk to the man on the west side of the town to begin the **Cave of Scriptures** sidequest. Head in. The girl here will tell us all the hints we need to complete this puzzle... assuming you're familiar with the Generation 3 Braille puzzles, that is.

The braille words all point to one of the other plaques in the room, and no plaque will repeat twice in a row. With some trial and error, you can therefore map out which characters correspond to which directions, "northwest", "southeast", "center", "northeast", and "southeast". ## Scorch Badge

Once you're ready for your first gym battle, talk to Connor in the town's battlefield!

!battle(["Connor",:SUPERNERD,0], "Grassy Terrain")

If you've studied up on the field notes for Grassy Terrain, you'll notice two key things: Fire-type moves are boosted on the field, and moves like Flame Burst will transform it into Burning Field in the absence of Rain or Water Sport. We don't really know what Burning Field is at this point, but suffice it to say that for a mono-fire type team, Burning Field is quite the advantage. Of course, the second clause of the above is a potential key to this fight: Poliwag, Surskit, Lombre, Buizel, and even Budew can all learn this move and use it to keep the Fire shenanigans to a minimum. While I'm sure it's possible, I simply don't recommend this fight without Water Sport. 

Making use of the Grassy Terrain yourself is not recommended, thanks to a lack of boost options that aren't weak to Fire. It really just comes down to overwhelming his Pokemon with super-effective moves and not getting overpowered by the Sun plus Burning Field combo. The Water type starters tend to be the best offensive answers here, though other Pokemon like Bibarel and Geodude can be very potent as well. Thanks to the ease of grinding on the Breeder, try out various team options here until you find the winning combination.

Winning grants us the **Scorch Badge** and brings our level cap up to Lv. 30! The Professor will also give us *TMX7 Rock Smash*.
